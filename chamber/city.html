Puente Alto

Puente Alto (1892-1960)

From its early years, the industrialization of Puente Alto attracted thousands of workers to work in its factories, who founded newspapers and unions to channel their demands. Its urban development was also impacted by the development of rail and road projects to connect the commune with Santiago, a process that was consolidated during the second half of the 20th century.

During the colony, the territory where Puente Alto is located today was a communication route between the Andes Mountains and the valley. Merchants, muleteers and gauchos circulated with cattle and merchandise through the commune. In addition, the lands that were supplied with irrigation after the canalization of the Maipo river in the 19th century, allowed the Puente Alto territory to experience an important agricultural development in this period, and especially of the wine industry. These lands were later divided into lots for sale, giving rise to the first streets of the town.
